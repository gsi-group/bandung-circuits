# Protected Terminology Glossary

Terms here must be **preserved exactly** when a speaker uses them — do not soften,
swap for euphemisms, or "balance" them away (see `references/voice-and-fidelity.md`,
section C). This is a *seed* list for Global South / movement / anti-imperialist
discourse. **Extend it per project** — add a project's names, acronyms, and
preferred spellings at the bottom so a draft stays consistent with house usage.

This list does NOT mean "insert these words." It means: *if the speaker used the
term (or a clear equivalent), keep it; never downgrade it.*

## Political-economic & analytical

- imperialism (not "geopolitical tension", "great-power competition")
- (neo)colonialism, settler-colonialism (not "the conflict", "historical legacy")
- occupation (not "presence", "administration")
- sovereignty, self-determination
- liberation, national liberation
- the Global South / the Third World (as the speakers use them)
- dependency, debt trap, structural adjustment, sanctions, blockade
- capital, accumulation, dispossession, extractivism
- non-alignment, multipolarity

## Movement & solidarity

- comrade, solidarity, internationalism
- the masses, the people, popular movements, grassroots
- struggle, resistance, mobilisation, organising
- people's assembly, front, campaign

## Charged descriptors (keep if the speaker used them — do not downgrade)

- genocide, ethnic cleansing, apartheid
- militarisation, military occupation
- repression, criminalisation, surveillance state

## Spelling / register conventions

- British spelling by default for institute/movement publications (organise,
  mobilisation, programme, centre, defence). Match the publication otherwise.

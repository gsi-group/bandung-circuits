# Meeting Minutes

**Meeting Title:** Hands Off Asia! Peace, Sovereignty, and Anti-US Militarism Webinar  
**Date:** April 30 (Vietnam Liberation Anniversary)  
**Duration:** Approximately 2 hours  
**Host Organizations:** International People's Assembly (IPA) and Tricontinental Institute for Social Research – Asia  
**Chair:** Tings Chak (Asia Co-Coordinator, Tricontinental Institute for Social Research)  
**Format:** Online webinar  
**Theme:** Peace, sovereignty, anti-militarism, and opposition to foreign military bases in Asia

## Meeting Overview

The webinar was held on the anniversary of Vietnam's liberation and reunification. Participants examined the historical and contemporary impact of U.S. military presence, military alliances, and intervention across Asia. Speakers from Vietnam, Iran, Okinawa (Japan), Taiwan, the Philippines, and South Korea shared perspectives on militarization, sovereignty, peace movements, and popular resistance.

The event also served as part of the launch of the **Hands Off Asia** campaign, which advocates for the removal of U.S. and NATO military bases from Asia, opposition to military blocs and aggressive military pacts, and respect for the sovereignty of Asian nations and peoples.

---

# Key Discussion Topics

## 1. Historical Lessons from Vietnam's Liberation Struggle

**Speaker:** Luna Nguyen (Vietnam)

Luna Nguyen reflected on the significance of April 30, 1975, when Vietnam achieved victory over the United States and reunified the country.

Key points included:

- The human cost of the Vietnam War, including millions of civilian and military deaths.
  
- The long-term consequences of Agent Orange contamination in Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia.
  
- The argument that U.S. military expansion has historically relied not only on direct military intervention but also on economic, diplomatic, and ideological influence.
  
- The importance of Vietnam's experience as evidence that organized popular resistance can defeat a militarily superior power.
  
- The relevance of anti-imperialist and anti-war struggles for younger generations today.
  

---

## 2. U.S. Military Bases and Regional Security in West Asia

**Speaker:** Setareh Sadeqi (University of Tehran, Iran)

Speaking from Iran, Setareh Sadeqi discussed the recent conflict involving Iran and the role of U.S. military infrastructure in the region.

Key points included:

- Personal observations from living in a region affected by military conflict.
  
- Criticism of the perception that U.S. military bases provide security for host countries.
  
- The argument that military bases can increase regional tensions and make host countries potential targets during conflicts.
  
- Discussion of U.S. and Israeli military actions and their impact on regional stability.
  
- The broader consequences of military intervention for civilian populations.
  

---

## 3. Okinawa: Military Occupation and Remilitarization

**Speaker:** Keiko Yonaha (No More Battle of Okinawa / Okinawa's Voice)

Keiko Yonaha presented the history of U.S. military occupation in Okinawa and ongoing grassroots resistance.

Key points included:

- Okinawa's disproportionate burden in hosting U.S. military facilities in Japan.
  
- The historical legacy of military occupation following World War II.
  
- Daily social, environmental, and political impacts of military bases on local communities.
  
- Long-term grassroots organizing against military expansion.
  
- Concerns about Japan's ongoing remilitarization and increased military spending.
  

---

## 4. Military and Ideological Influence in Taiwan

**Speaker:** Professor Chen Meixian (National Cheng Kung University)

Professor Chen discussed military and ideological dimensions of U.S. influence in Taiwan.

Key points included:

- The strategic position of Taiwan within regional geopolitical tensions.
  
- The relationship between military policies and ideological narratives.
  
- Concerns regarding militarization and its effects on cross-strait relations.
  
- The importance of public awareness and critical examination of dominant security narratives.
  
- Shared experiences between Taiwan and Okinawa regarding military presence and regional security concerns.
  

---

## 5. The Philippines and the Global Anti-Base Movement

**Speaker:** Corazon Valdes-Fabros (International Peace Bureau)

Corazon Valdes-Fabros discussed the Philippines' historical relationship with U.S. military bases and broader international peace movements.

Key points included:

- The role of U.S. bases in supporting military operations during the Vietnam War.
  
- The successful campaign in the 1990s that led to the removal of major U.S. military bases from the Philippines.
  
- Concerns regarding the return and expansion of military facilities in the country.
  
- The importance of linking local struggles with international peace movements.
  
- Lessons learned from decades of anti-base organizing.
  

---

## 6. International Solidarity and Movement Building

**Speaker:** Chongmun (South Korea)

The final speaker emphasized the importance of building international solidarity against militarism.

Key points included:

- The need to communicate anti-war and anti-imperialist perspectives beyond activist circles.
  
- The importance of public education and grassroots mobilization.
  
- Building connections among movements across countries.
  
- Expanding participation through rallies, campaigns, and public engagement.
  
- Strengthening international cooperation in confronting militarization.
  

---

# Decisions Made

### Launch of the Hands Off Asia Campaign

Participants formally supported the launch and promotion of the **Hands Off Asia** campaign.

The campaign advocates:

- Removal of U.S. and NATO military bases from Asia.
  
- Opposition to military alliances and military escalation in the region.
  
- Defense of national sovereignty and self-determination.
  
- Greater international solidarity among peace movements.
  

### Continued Regional Engagement

Organizers indicated that this webinar would be the beginning of an ongoing series of discussions and educational activities focused on militarism, sovereignty, and resistance movements in Asia.

# Open Questions

Several issues remained open for further discussion:

1. How can anti-militarist movements more effectively reach and mobilize younger generations?
   
2. What strategies are most effective for building regional and international solidarity across diverse political contexts?
   
3. How can activists counter both military and ideological forms of influence and intervention?
   
4. What practical steps can communities take to challenge military expansion in their own countries?
   
5. How can existing peace movements coordinate more effectively across Asia and the Global South?
